Proposal

This assignment was an adapted version of UCF's Office of Undergraduate Research Student Research Grant, in which I had to follow a specific set of guidelines. My proposal was "Plastic invading Mosquitos: Quantifying the Atmospheric and Waterborne Microplastic Arriving at the Mosquito Lagoon." I pitched it under Dr. Linda J. Walters, who is a very notable researcher in this field.

Document

Reflection

The method consists of different water and air collectors across the shore, sampled monthly for 3 to 5 months during the Fall 2026 semester. The stakes are real: if we find out that atmospheric input is significant, stopping the input from water sources might not be enough. This could lead to safety issues regarding the consumption of food harvested from these places. This matters to me personally because I've been following microplastics for some time and the damage they can cause to the human body. It's also scary because the current science says these microplastics will cause generational harm.
