About me

I am a CS student at UCF, focused on build-and-ship to see the fruits of my labor.

As a student at UCF I have taken various CS classes to advance my career — CAP 4611 (Machine Learning), CIS 3360 (Cybersecurity), and the CS 1 & 2 sequence.

I pass my time working on random projects, usually following trends since it's what pulls me in the most. I've worked on an OpenClaw project where I set up an agent to classify expenses from the Plaid API into a spreadsheet, separated by accounting periods, and offer recommendations. In that same framework I used the Canvas API to map onto my Logseq notebook, so every journal entry gets paired with assignments, due dates, and class info. My projects are born from my inconveniences.

I work mostly in Python, C, C++, Java, and JavaScript, with HTML and CSS when a project needs a front-end. For data, I use pandas, scikit-learn, and stuff like k-means. My daily tools are Git, OrbStack, and Logseq. I also speak English and Spanish, and I'm casually learning Japanese.
